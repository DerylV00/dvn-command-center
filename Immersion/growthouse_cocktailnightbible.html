01 · Production Overview

What Cocktail Night Actually Is

Cocktail Night is a separate production from the Growth House Immersion. It has its own team, its own run of show, its own operations lead (still Deryl), and its own Production Bible — this one. The fact that it happens on the same property on the last night of the weekend does not make it a dinner party. It is an event.

When
Last night of the Growth House Immersion weekend. After the work is done. The celebration belongs at the end. Confirmed by Jesse April 23.
Venue
Same property as the Immersion for now. Every April locked to the same venue. As the event scales, venue flexibility increases.
Guest List
Growth House members plus external cocktail night guests. Members are VIP. Cocktail night is the exception where the energy opens beyond the tight immersion circle.
Scale Vision
Starting at current member count. Designed to scale to 150–200+ as the brand grows. Possibly at a separate venue (dealership, rooftop, etc.) in future years.
» Jesse's words: "I think we all agree that should be the last closing thing." This is the celebration. It should feel like one.

What Makes This Night Different

•DJ — live music energy. Sets the arc from arrival through close. This is the first thing members feel when they walk into the space.
•Security — professional perimeter. Guest list managed. Members and sponsors never feel the friction of it.
•Three bars — cocktail, wine/beer, non-alc sponsor. Opposite sides of the space to split traffic and create movement.
•Photo booth — branded activation. Content machine. Memory maker. Operator actively pulls guests in.
•Two photographers — Amys plus a second. Every zone covered. Every moment captured. Sponsor product always in frame.
•Sponsor activations — product in use, natural placement. Sponsor intros executed in first 90 minutes by Deryl.
•QR capture — non-member guests directed to intake form at bar and entrance. Every guest becomes a lead.

Zone Rules

•U-shape containment: All energy stays within the defined zone. No guests past the boundary markers. Jesse confirmed this in the April 23 call.
•Cocktail bar and wine/beer bar on opposite sides — this is deliberate. It creates movement, splits crowds, and makes the space feel full from every angle.
•Directional signage at every bar and both bathrooms — guests never stand still wondering where to go.
•Couches removed from main living room — confirmed with Jesse. Open floor for movement and conversation.
•Photo booth positioned mid-space — visible from every zone, accessible from all directions, operator always in motion.
•Pool zone — open but managed. Security covers perimeter. Floor staff rotates through. No dead zones.

07 · Bar System

Three-Bar System

Three bars. Opposite sides of the space. Every type of guest has their own lane. No bottlenecks. Triple the sponsor visibility.

Bar 1 — Signature Cocktail Bar

🍹The Go-Giver: Dr. Goodburn's Tequila + Sly BOOST Tropical + Simple Syrup + Cranberry. Garnish: orange slice + cherry.
🍹Mint-Multiplier: Bourbon + Sly CHILL Citrus + Simple Syrup + Bitters. Garnish: cherry.
🍹The Legacy: Bourbon + Sly CHILL Mango + Simple Syrup + Bitters. Garnish: grapefruit + rosemary.
🍹Espresso Martini: Vodka + espresso + coffee liqueur + simple syrup. AZ lesson: stock sufficient vodka — minimum 3 bottles.
⚠ Arizona gaps: Vodka too light (1 bottle), Red Bull too light (2 units), grapefruit + rosemary missing for The Legacy. Fix before October.

Bar 2 — Wine & Beer

•Full wine selection — red, white, sparkling, champagne
•Beer selection — curated, not exhaustive. Quality over volume.
•Champagne managed here for toasts — Deryl coordinates timing
•~1.5 bottles wine per member as baseline. Adjust with actuals.

Bar 3 — Non-Alc Sponsor Activation

•Sly CHILL / BOOST as the anchor product — describes the benefits, not just the flavors
•Mocktail menu: built with Deryl pre-event. At least 3 options.
•Premium water — sparkling and still. Never just tap.
•Photo-ready setup — sponsor product placed prominently for shots

Universal Bar Rules

•Full mixer list confirmed with every bartender in pre-event call — Coke, soda water, tonic, simple syrup, citrus. Never assumed.
•Ice: machine + backup bags. Always order backup. Tell Deryl immediately if running low.
•Backup ice is non-negotiable regardless of machine status.
•Per-member bar quantity formula built from AZ actuals before October.

09 · Sponsor Strategy

Sponsor Strategy

Sponsors integrate through product, placement, and a warm introduction. Never through a stage presentation.

Dr. Goodburn's Tequila
Cocktail bar anchor. The Go-Giver cocktail built around their product. Pair with spirits, hospitality, and lifestyle members in introductions.
Sly
Non-alc bar anchor. Mocktail menu built on their product. Pair with health, wellness, and performance members. Wellness window during the day primes the introduction at night.
New Sponsor Criteria
Aligns with the group's wellness and performance identity. Integration should feel organic — product use, placement, warm introduction. Zero stage presentations required.

Introduction Protocol

•2–3 intentional warm sponsor-to-member introductions in first 90 minutes
•Pairs pre-mapped during prep week — loaded into Deryl's AI agent, fires as reminders during the event
•Introduction style: warm, personal, context-specific. "James — you should meet [SPONSOR]. They're building something you'd find interesting because…"
•Treat sponsors like VIP members, not vendors. They came back because of relationship.
•Sponsor offset documented as a budget line item after every event
