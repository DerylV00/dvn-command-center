The Immersion Structure

Six distinct days. Each with its own team configuration, energy, and operational requirements. The cocktail night is a separate production — see the Cocktail Night Production Bible.

07 · Member Hospitality

Member Hospitality

Every member should feel the event was designed specifically for them. That feeling is manufactured deliberately — through intake, preparation, and intentional execution.

» A room is a message. It either says 'we thought about you' or 'we set up a bed.' We are always saying the first.

Member Intake — Probing Questions Format

5–7 questions. Feels like a conversation. Takes 3 minutes. No pamphlets. Sent 3 weeks out.

•Dietary restrictions or allergies — hard stops only
•Exact coffee order and morning drink preference
•Wellness preferences — sauna, cold plunge, yoga, morning person or not
•Favorite snacks and drinks — what should be waiting in your room?
•One thing you're working through right now (optional — powerful for Jesse's session planning)
•Anything that would make this weekend feel perfect for you

09 · Emergency Protocols

Emergency Protocols

The best production teams aren't the ones who never face problems — they're the ones who already decided what to do before the problem happened.

⚠ All emergency contacts saved in Deryl's phone before every event. Nearest hospital, urgent care, venue emergency line confirmed 48 hours out.

Scenario 1 — Chef Cancels Within 48 Hours

→Deryl notifies Jesse privately. Do not announce to members.
→Option A: Backup chef — identify one before every event. Have the number saved.
→Option B: Premium catering delivery — 2 options researched for October venue city before the event.
→Jesse addresses any meal change to members as a curated decision, not a crisis.

Scenario 2 — AV Fails During a Session

→Joshawn owns the fix — quietly. Jesse continues speaking, holds room energy.
→If fix takes more than 3 minutes: Jesse calls a comfort break, framed as intentional.
→Backup device charged and ready. Switch to it immediately.
→If unresolvable: Jesse continues without slides. A great facilitator doesn't need a deck.

Scenario 3 — Member Medical Situation

→Floor staff reports to Deryl immediately — do not handle alone.
→If non-emergency: Deryl handles discreetly. Jesse informed privately, not mid-session.
→If emergency: Call 911 immediately. Deryl stays with member. Jesse notified.
→Member emergency contact is on their profile card. Deryl has it.

Scenario 4 — Key Staff No-Show

→Joshawn no-show: Deryl takes AV station temporarily. Load simplest possible setup.
→Floor staff no-show: Remaining staff absorbs zones. Deryl adjusts rotation. Service may simplify but never visibly degrades.
→Rule: no member ever notices a staff gap.
