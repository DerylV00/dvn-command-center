Cocktail Night · Full production · Nate's house
The Transition Moment
Mountain → Mansion.
The 2-Hour Drive That Becomes Part of the Story.

The convoy from Sedona/Flagstaff to Scottsdale is roughly 2 hours. Frame it intentionally — debrief the mountain days in the vehicles, set the intention for the final night. Arrive as a unit, already bonded, already energized. The mansion lit up in blue and purple hits like a reward for three days of real work. Nobody who does that transition forgets it.

Why the Mountain Changes Everything
•The status layer disappears. Nobody has a competitive advantage building a fire. Nobody's business wins matter on an ATV trail. The equality creates connection that a session room can never manufacture.
•The 11pm campfire conversation is irreplaceable. It cannot be programmed or facilitated. It just happens when fire, exhaustion from a physical day, and people who've spent 48 hours doing real work together all coincide.
•Jesse can go deeper. When there's nowhere else to be, when phones don't work, when the environment strips everything back — the conversations in sessions go to a different level.
•The contrast earns the celebration. The cocktail night hits differently when you've spent three days raw and real. The pool lit up, the DJ, the speakeasy reveal — these feel like a reward that was earned.
Mountain Venue Requirements
•Enough bedrooms for the full group — sleep-in format is non-negotiable. Members driving back to hotels each night breaks the continuity entirely.
•Outdoor fire pit — the fire is where it happens. This is not optional.
•Land for activities — on-property or immediately adjacent. ATVs, hiking, fishing. The physical day needs the physical space.
•A kitchen Chef Shannon can work in — or the option to cook over fire for certain meals, which is its own experience.
•Limited cell signal — this is a feature, not a problem. Members being unreachable from their business for 48 hours is part of what makes the immersion work.
NIGHT
Format C · The Long Game · 2028+

The Standalone
Night.

Cocktail Night becomes its own brand. Its own ticket. Its own guest list that extends beyond the mastermind. People in the Arizona business community are asking how to get in who have never heard of Growth House. That is the goal.

Separate venue option · Own identity · Scales to 200+
What Changes in Format C
•Cocktail Night gets its own name and brand identity. Not "the Growth House cocktail party." A named event that has its own reputation, its own aesthetic, its own annual identity.
•The guest list expands independently. Growth House members still attend. But the wider guest list is Arizona entrepreneurs, community figures, sponsors, and their networks. 150–200+ people.
•Venue moves if the event outgrows the mansion. A car dealership showroom, a rooftop venue, a private club. Still potentially Nate's house for the mastermind — but cocktail night might need its own space as it scales.
•It becomes a revenue line. Sponsor packages at this scale have real value. Tickets for non-members. The event funds itself and contributes to Growth House's bottom line.
•Deryl's role is Event Director of the night, not just Operations. At this scale the cocktail night is a separate production that Deryl owns end-to-end. It is his flagship event within the Growth House brand.
