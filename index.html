MODELS

These aren't concepts to know — they're lenses to see through. The difference between someone who reads books and someone who changes their life is whether the mental models become automatic. Use these daily.

01
WHO BEFORE HOW
Source: Blaze Genius / Dan Sullivan
Before you ask "how do I do this?", ask "who has already done this?" or "who can open this door for me?" Your genius is in relationship leverage, not process execution. Blaze types who ask HOW first exhaust themselves. Blaze types who ask WHO first build empires.
Every week: what's one WHO you haven't called yet?
02
SHADOW → GIFT
Source: Gene Keys · Richard Rudd
Every shadow (Chaos, Discord, Interference) contains its gift on the other side. The shadow isn't the enemy — it's the doorway. When you're in Key 3 Chaos (scattered, overwhelmed, can't focus), the gift is Innovation just on the other side of that friction. Name the shadow. Walk through it, don't run from it.
When feeling chaos: "What is the gift in this?" Then act from Gift, not Shadow.
03
THE GOLDEN CIRCLE
Source: Start With Why · Simon Sinek
WHY → HOW → WHAT. Lead every conversation, every pitch, every piece of content from the inside out. Start with belief. Most people start with what they do (the product, the service, the resume). You start with why you do it — and that creates a completely different kind of attention and trust.
Before every conversation: what's my WHY today? Start there.
04
COMPOUND INTEREST OF CHARACTER
Source: Naval Ravikant / Almanac
Long-term games with long-term people. In long-term games, trust compounds. In short-term games, everything resets. The bar regulars who see you every week — that's a compound interest account. Ken Wilkins over 4 years — compound interest. Every person you treat well becomes a node that eventually sends value back.
Every interaction: am I playing a long-term or short-term game here?
05
THE GO-GIVER LAW
Source: The Go-Giver · Bob Burg
Your influence is determined by how abundantly you place other people's interests first. The paradox: the less you try to get, the more you receive. The more you focus on adding value to others without tracking what you're owed, the more flows back. This is the opposite of how most people operate at work, in sales, and in relationships.
Daily question: how did I add value without asking for anything today?
06
SPECIFIC KNOWLEDGE
Source: Naval Ravikant
Specific knowledge is what you know that can't be trained — it's built from your unique combination of experience, curiosity, and personality. Your specific knowledge: lake hospitality culture, consciousness + psychedelics, immersion event production, ENFJ people-reading, servant leadership in food & beverage. Nobody else has exactly this combination. It's the moat.
Build at the intersection nobody else occupies. That's where the leverage is.
07
MOTIVATION MAPPING
Source: Laws of Human Nature · Greene
People are not motivated by what they say they want. They're motivated by something deeper — an identity they're moving toward or away from, a fear they won't name, a desire they're afraid to admit. Your job is to find the deeper motivation and align with it. This is what Gene Key 13 listening enables: you hear what they're not saying.
In every relationship: what's the real motivation beneath the stated one?
08
ENVIRONMENT IS DESTINY
Source: Outwitting the Devil · Hill
98% of people drift — they move through life without a definite aim, absorbing the environment they're in without choosing it. The remaining 2% design their environment deliberately. Drifters are made by circumstances; non-drifters make their circumstances. Your current environment at Buddy's has drift built into it. Name it. Counteract it. Build your own.
Weekly: what am I absorbing from my environment? Is it building or eroding?
09
THE SERVANT-LEADER PARADOX
Source: Go-Giver / Greenleaf / Multiple
The leader who serves rises highest. Not because serving is a strategy — but because genuine service creates the deepest loyalty, the most resilient teams, and the most authentic influence. People don't follow titles. They follow people who make them feel seen, capable, and cared for. You do this instinctively. Now do it with intention and scale it.
In every leadership moment: am I creating conditions for others to thrive, or just directing?

Action in alignment with natural flow produces more than forced effort. When something feels like strain — when you're pushing and pushing and nothing gives — that's usually a signal you're fighting the current. The Blaze genius in flow IS wu wei.
